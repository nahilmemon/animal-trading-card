Arctic wolves have two layers of fur in order to survive in the bitter cold.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus arctos
  • Average Length: 3.3-6 ft (1-1.8 m)
  • Average Lifespan: 7-10 years in the wild
  • Habitat: Tundra

Arctic wolves are the only species of wolves that are almost completely white. They live mostly in Alaska, but small populations live in Canada and Greenland as well. They have small ears to help regulate their body temperature and padded paws to allow them good grip while walking on icy surfaces.
